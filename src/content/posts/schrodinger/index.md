Hello, traveler of knowledge~ This is **Chemistry Mastery Nahida**.

## Schrödinger's thought: from matter waves

Schrodinger is first inspired by de Broglie's matter waves.  
In case of de Broglie showed particles can act as waves, what are the waves?  

De Broglie has reported the idea of particles having wave and frequency:  
$$E=h\nu,~p=h/\lambda$$
According to de Broglie's report, the wave can be written by complex form:  
$$\psi=A \exp (\frac{i}{\hbar}(p\cdot x-Et))$$  
and the real form:  
$$\psi=c_{1}\sin (\frac{1}{\hbar}(p\cdot x-Et)) + c_{2}\cos (\frac{1}{\hbar}(p\cdot x-Et))$$  

## Trial to applying relativity (1925)

Schrödinger first attempted to get relativistic equation.  
From relativistic energy-momentum relation:  
$$E^{2}=p^{2}c^{2}+m^{2}c^{4}$$
